## TL;DR for the VP

**Spring EQ's DSCR program is a "prime-credit DSCR" niche, not a head-on
Deephaven competitor.** Our matrix is materially tighter than Deephaven's
on borrower scope (no FN, no ITIN, no first-time investors, US-citizen/PR
only), property scope (no STR via condotel ban, no rural, no >5 acres,
no non-warrantable condos), and structure (no layered entities, $500k
cash-out cap, manual UW only, all appraisals lender-ordered). Deephaven
is structurally better positioned to scale volume; we are structurally
better positioned to print clean securitizations.

**Two strategic options follow from the data:**
- **Path A (recommended for launch):** stay niche, beat Deephaven by
  25–50 bps **only in the cells where our matrix overlaps theirs**
  (700+ FICO, 1.00+ DSCR, vanilla SFR/2-4 unit, US citizens/PRs).
  Compete on price and execution quality, not breadth.
- **Path B (multi-quarter product roadmap):** systematically plug the
  blindsides — add FN, STR-native, first-time investor, layered entity
  vesting. Each expansion is a separate legal/compliance/repricing
  exercise.

## The four-dimension grading

### 1. Where Deephaven is entrenched against us

These are positions we cannot displace within the current program design.
Don't fight — design around.

1. **Foreign National investor segment.** They have a program; we have a
   ban. Latin American + Asian capital flowing into US rentals goes to
   them by default.
2. **ITIN borrowers.** Same forfeit.
3. **First-time investors (BiggerPockets generation).** Their permitted-
   with-LTV-cap policy vs. our outright ban. This is one of the fastest-
   growing investor segments. We don't book any of it.
4. **Big-ticket investors ($2.5M+ loans).** Our matrix tops out where
   theirs continues. High-NW investor relationships flow to them.
5. **STR investors.** They take 25–50 bps and book it; our condotel ban
   plus long-term-lease-only DSCR calc forces STR investors to walk.
6. **Sophisticated structures (layered entities, holdco vesting,
   subordinate financing).** They permit; we don't.
7. **Speed-to-quote in broker channel.** Their IDENTI-FI AUS in Encompass
   returns decisions in 1–3 minutes. We are manual UW with no AUS.
8. **Big cash-out (BRRRR strategy).** Our $500k cap closes the door on
   the most aggressive portfolio investors.
9. **Servicing integration via Selene.** ~5–10 bps of execution
   efficiency we can't match without buying or partnering with a servicer.
10. **DRMT securitization shelf.** Multiple completed deals = investor
    familiarity = tighter senior spreads. We start that clock at zero.
11. **Stacked product line (HELOC + DSCR 2nd + DSCR 1st).** They sell a
    ladder; we sell a rung.

### 2. Spring EQ blindsides Deephaven exploits today

Same list as #1, restated as our own structural exposure. Specifically
worth flagging to product:

- **No FN, ITIN, or non-permanent-resident program** — three forfeited
  segments stacked.
- **First-time investors banned outright** — defines as "no mortgage in
  past 3 years," which excludes many individual investors who own only
  their primary residence. This is too tight.
- **Long-term-lease-only DSCR calc** — STR income (Airbnb / VRBO platform
  statements) cannot be underwritten in this program, full stop.
- **$500k cash-out hard cap** — eliminates large-portfolio refi business.
- **Layered-entity ban** — eliminates investors who hold via parent-LLC
  / family LLC structures, which is the norm above ~$1M net worth.
- **Manual UW + lender-ordered appraisal + transfer ban** = ~7–10 days
  slower than Deephaven and 14–21 days slower than fintech-style DSCR
  shops. Brokers feel this on every file.
- **No published rate sheet / wholesale portal in this guidelines doc.**
  Means brokers can't self-quote. They have to call.
- **No DSCR Second / DSCR HELOC** — leaves cross-sell / pull-through
  revenue on the table that Deephaven captures.

### 3. Inertia working in our favor (assets to leverage)

What Spring EQ already has that Deephaven doesn't:

1. **Spring EQ as a HELOC / home-equity brand.** Brokers know our name
   from second-mortgage / equity origination. Cross-sell into DSCR is a
   warm-start that Deephaven cannot replicate without acquiring a HELOC
   shop.
2. **PA HQ closer to NY/NJ/MA broker hubs** than Charlotte. Easier to
   build East Coast wholesale relationships ground-up.
3. **MO + WV licensing** — coverage Deephaven cannot claim.
4. **Cleaner credit book.** 660 FICO floor + 4-yr seasoning + zero-
   tolerance on 12mo mortgage lates + tax-liens-paid-or-no = a higher-
   quality DSCR pool than Deephaven's mixed-credit book. **A future
   securitization off our shelf should price tighter than DRMT** when
   we have enough volume to print.
5. **DSCR 0.75x floor with clean LTV grid published.** Many lenders
   bury sub-ratio rules; we publish 0.75–0.99x as a defined product cell
   (FICO 700+, $1.5M cap, 70/60 LTV). This is broker-friendly and
   investor-defensible.
6. **Manual UW as a feature.** Position as "every file gets a senior
   underwriter" — this is a real broker pitch for off-script deals
   where AUS would say no.
7. **Investment-only focus, no consumer non-QM overhang.** No QM/ATR
   compliance entanglements. Faster product changes.
8. **Guidelines dated April 2026.** Current. Deephaven's publicly indexed
   matrix PDFs are 12/2023 and 02/2024 — stale.

### 4. Where we are vulnerable to a disruptive threat

Beyond Deephaven specifically — the program as written is exposed to:

1. **Any new entrant offering FN + ITIN + first-time + STR + layered
   entities at competitive pricing.** Five blindsides stacked = an easy
   pitch deck for a competitor.
2. **AUS-driven fintechs (Loan Market, Stessa-integrated lenders, etc.)
   that return decisions in <60 seconds.** Our manual UW + transferred-
   appraisal ban makes us look antique to broker-operations leads.
3. **Anyone with $1M+ cash-out** for BRRRR-strategy investors.
4. **Anyone offering 1-day appraisal transfer + product approval.**
   Brokers love deals where they don't have to re-order.

### Where we can disrupt — given current program

Realistic 6–12 month moves that don't require ripping up the matrix:

1. **NY licensing.** Both we and Deephaven miss this. ~10% of national
   non-QM volume. First mover in NY DSCR wins. Confirm NY PPP-restriction
   compatibility with our 1/3/5yr structures.
2. **Publish a wholesale rate sheet with PPP-buyout pricing.** Deephaven
   doesn't (publicly). Transparency is a broker-acquisition tool.
3. **Cross-sell from Spring EQ HELOC originator base.** Every active
   HELOC broker is a candidate to send DSCR. No new acquisition cost.
4. **Position the manual-UW + clean-credit book as "prime DSCR".**
   Target the broker who wants the deal that *will* securitize cleanly,
   not the deal that fits a credit-flexible matrix. Sell the secondary-
   market story.
5. **Beat Deephaven by 25–50 bps in the 700–739 / 70–75 LTV / 1.20+ DSCR
   overlap cell** at launch. This is the one cell where our matrix
   competes head-on, and our cleaner book funds the discount through
   tighter eventual securitization economics.
6. **Add MO + WV branding to "lower 48 minus HI"** — coverage parity
   talking point.

### Where we should NOT try to disrupt

- Don't chase FN, ITIN, first-time investor, or STR within this
  program. Each is a separate product build.
- Don't chase $3M+ loan size. Deephaven's matrix is structurally bigger.
- Don't chase 14-day close until manual-UW + lender-appraisal policy
  changes. We can't ship that promise.
